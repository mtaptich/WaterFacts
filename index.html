So, what can you do to reduce your water footprint in these notably dry times?

Well, you should begin your conservation efforts by breaking this particular question down into three major parts:

  1. How much water do I directly consume and use?
  2. How much water is needed to produce the resources I consume?
  3. Knowing this, how can I most effectively conserve water??

It is easy to monitor the amount of water you directy consume and use and regulate accordingly. Spending less time in the shower, watering your landscape less often, and detecting and fixing leaky faucets are ways to conserve water at home. However, for the last two questions, you need a more scientific approach for this type of 'water accounting.' Though often forgotten, the last these two questions are very important for truly reducing the total amount of water you use daily.

Water resource engineers and scientists will employ a or LCA to estimate the total water demand for a produce, service, or infrastructure system. LCA requires the processing of large datasets in generating valuable results to aid in selecting between alternatives. Academics and private companies have utilized LCA for decades in sustainability analyses.
